# 15 - ICT Glossary

**Complete alphabetical reference of Inner Circle Trader terminology.**

---

## A

**Accumulation**
Phase of AMD pattern where price consolidates in a range while institutions build positions. Usually occurs during Asian session (8 PM - 12 AM EST).

**AMD Pattern**
Accumulation-Manipulation-Distribution. The daily cycle where price consolidates, sweeps liquidity, then moves in the true direction.

**Asia Range**
The price range established during Asian session (8 PM - 12 AM EST). Marked by Asia High and Asia Low, used for AMD trading.

**AVCC**
Analog VCC voltage reading from motherboard sensors. Typically shows 3.3V rail status.

---

## B

**BISI**
Buy Side Imbalance. A bullish Fair Value Gap where price gaps up, creating a buying opportunity below current price.

**Breaker Block (BR)**
An Order Block that was broken and now acts as the opposite type (bullish OB becomes bearish resistance).

**BOS**
Break of Structure. When price breaks beyond the previous swing point in the direction of the trend, confirming continuation.

**BSL**
Buy Side Liquidity. Pools of orders sitting above price (EQH, swing highs) that fuel upward moves.

---

## C

**CBDR**
Central Bank Dealing Range. The range formed between 2:00-5:00 AM EST during London session when central banks execute orders.

**CE**
Consequent Encroachment. The 50% level of a Fair Value Gap, often where price returns to fill the imbalance.

**CHoCH**
Change of Character. When price breaks a swing point against the established trend direction, signaling potential reversal.

**Confluence**
When multiple factors align at the same price level (OTE + FVG + OB), increasing probability.

---

## D

**Dealing Range**
The range between a significant swing high and low, divided into premium and discount zones.

**Discount Zone**
The lower half of a dealing range (0-50%), considered "cheap" prices and good for buying.

**Distribution**
Phase of AMD pattern where price moves in the true direction with momentum, usually during NY session (10-11 AM EST).

---

## E

**EQH**
Equal Highs. Two or more swing highs at approximately the same price level, creating liquidity above price.

**EQL**
Equal Lows. Two or more swing lows at approximately the same price level, creating liquidity below price.

**External Liquidity**
Liquidity outside the dealing range (swing highs/lows, EQH, EQL) that price seeks before major moves.

---

## F

**Fair Value Gap (FVG)**
An imbalance in price created when price moves aggressively, leaving a gap between candles. Price often returns to fill it.

**Fresh OB**
An Order Block that has never been tested since it formed. Highest probability.

**FVG**
See Fair Value Gap.

---

## G

**Golden Ratio**
61.8% Fibonacci retracement level. Key level for OTE calculations.

---

## H

**HH**
Higher High. A swing high that is higher than the previous swing high in an uptrend.

**HL**
Higher Low. A swing low that is higher than the previous swing low in an uptrend.

**HTF**
Higher Time Frame. Usually Daily, 4H, or 1H charts used for establishing bias.

---

## I

**ICT**
Inner Circle Trader. The trading methodology created by Michael J. Huddleston.

**IL**
Internal Liquidity. Liquidity within the dealing range (FVGs, OBs, dealing range levels) used as profit targets.

**Inducement**
A fake liquidity level created to induce retail traders to enter before being swept.

**Internal Liquidity**
See IL.

---

## K

**Killzone**
High-probability time windows when institutional algorithms are most active. London (3-5 AM) and NY (10-11 AM).

**LKZ**
London Killzone. 3:00-5:00 AM EST. High probability window for breakouts and trend establishment.

---

## L

**LH**
Lower High. A swing high that is lower than the previous swing high in a downtrend.

**LL**
Lower Low. A swing low that is lower than the previous swing low in a downtrend.

**Liquidity**
Pools of orders (stops, breakout orders) that price seeks to fill before making major moves.

**LTF**
Lower Time Frame. Usually 15M, 5M, or 1M charts used for precision entries.

---

## M

**Manipulation**
Phase of AMD pattern where price sweeps Asia High or Low to trigger stops before reversing.

**MB**
Mitigation Block. An Order Block that has been tested but held, still valid for second entry.

**Mean Threshold (MT)**
The 50% midpoint of a dealing range, often acting as support/resistance.

**Mitigation Block**
See MB.

---

## N

**NYKZ**
New York Killzone. 10:00-11:00 AM EST. Highest probability window for trend continuation.

---

## O

**OB**
Order Block. The last opposing candle before a strong impulse, representing institutional order accumulation.

**OTE**
Optimal Trade Entry. The 50-62% retracement zone where price typically pulls back before continuing.

**OSOK**
One Shot One Kill. A setup with 7+ confluence factors, extremely high probability.

---

## P

**PB**
Propulsion Block. An Order Block that created extreme momentum, indicating aggressive institutional activity.

**PDH**
Previous Day High. External liquidity level above price.

**PDL**
Previous Day Low. External liquidity level below price.

**PO3**
Power of 3. Same as AMD pattern (Accumulation-Manipulation-Distribution).

**Premium Zone**
The upper half of a dealing range (50-100%), considered "expensive" prices and good for selling.

---

## R

**Reclaim**
When price breaks a level but then closes back above/below it, validating a Turtle Soup setup.

**R:R**
Risk to Reward ratio. Minimum 1:2 required for most ICT setups.

---

## S

**SIBI**
Sell Side Imbalance. A bearish Fair Value Gap where price gaps down, creating a selling opportunity above current price.

**Silver Bullet**
The 20-minute window (9:50-10:10 AM EST) with highest probability setups.

**SMT**
Smart Money Tool (or Smart Money Technique). Using correlated asset divergence to confirm or warn about price moves.

**SSL**
Sell Side Liquidity. Pools of orders sitting below price (EQL, swing lows) that fuel downward moves.

**Structure**
The pattern of swing highs and lows (HH, HL or LH, LL) that defines trend direction.

**Sweep**
When price briefly breaks a level (EQH, EQL, Asia High/Low) to trigger stops before reversing.

**Swing High**
A candle high that is higher than the candles immediately before and after it.

**Swing Low**
A candle low that is lower than the candles immediately before and after it.

---

## T

**Turtle Soup**
A setup trading the false breakout (liquidity sweep) that immediately reverses.

---

## V

**VCC**
Voltage sensor reading, typically showing 3.3V, 5V, or 12V rail status from motherboard.
