## ğŸ¯ What is an Order Block?

An Order Block is the last opposing candle before a strong impulse move. It represents:
- Where institutional orders were accumulated
- A zone of significant buying or selling interest
- A level where price is likely to react when revisited

**ICT's Definition:**
> "Order Blocks are the last bearish candle before a bullish impulse, or the last bullish candle before a bearish impulse. They represent where smart money entered the market."

### HTF vs LTF OBs

**HTF OBs (4H, Daily):**
- Hold more significance
- Provide major support/resistance
- Can last for days/weeks
- Priority over LTF OBs

- More frequent
- Provide precision entry
- Form within HTF OBs
- Confirm HTF analysis

## ğŸ¯ OB Entry Strategies

### Strategy 1: Direct OB Entry
```
1. Identify fresh OB
2. Place limit order at 50% of OB
3. Stop at OB extreme
4. Target next liquidity

Aggressive: Enter at OB edge
Conservative: Wait for confirmation candle
```

### Strategy 2: OB + Confirmation
```
1. Price reaches OB zone
2. Wait for bullish/bearish candle
3. Enter on confirmation
4. Stop below/above OB

Confirmation types:
- Engulfing candle
- Pin bar
- Inside bar breakout
```

### Strategy 3: OB Breaker Play
```
1. OB breaks (violated)
2. Wait for retest from new side
3. Enter with the break
4. Stop beyond original OB

Example:
- Bullish OB breaks down
- Wait for pullback to OB from below
- Short at retest
- Stop above OB high
```

## ğŸ‹ï¸ Practical Exercises for Mastery

1.  **Fresh OB Hunt:**
    *   On a 1H chart, scroll back in time until you find a large, impulsive move.
    *   Identify the Order Block that initiated this move. Mark its high, low, and 50% level.
    *   Play the chart forward candle by candle. Did price return to the OB? If so, how did it react? Was the 50% level respected? Repeat this 10 times to build a feel for fresh OBs.

2.  **Breaker Block Flip:**
    *   Go to a 15M chart and find an area where a clear support or resistance level failed.
    *   Look for an Order Block that was clearly violated. Mark this as a potential Breaker Block.
    *   Observe if price came back to retest the *other side* of this violated OB. This exercise helps in understanding the polarity flip concept.

3.  **HTF to LTF Order Block Drill:**
    *   Find and mark a clear Order Block on a 4H chart.
    *   Switch to a 15M chart for that same time period.
    *   Look for smaller, more refined 15M Order Blocks that exist *within the range* of the 4H OB. This shows how timeframes work together and helps you find more precise entries.
